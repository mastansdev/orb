12. Final Principle
The Brain should think like a Portfolio Manager.

The ORB should behave like a Dealer.

The Broker should behave like an Executor.

Each component has one responsibility.

Add a Memory Layer.

Not chat memory—market memory.

It sits alongside the Brain and answers questions like:

"This sector has been leading for the last 5 trading days."
"This stock has already failed three ORB breakouts today."
"Government announced a policy yesterday—its effects are still unfolding."
"Results season is ongoing for this industry."

That gives the Brain context across time, instead of treating every tick as independent.

I think that memory layer will become one of the biggest differentiators between a reactive trading bot and an institutional decision system.


Rule-001

ORB is the default execution strategy.

The Brain may override ORB only when an
Exceptional Opportunity exists.

Exceptional Opportunity is determined by
institutional-grade evidence, never by price alone.

nstead of calling it "ORB Override", call it "Execution Strategy Selection."

That keeps the architecture clean.

The Brain is not breaking a rule—it is selecting the most appropriate execution method for the market conditions.

On 95% of trading days, the selector will choose ORB.

On the rare days with major policy announcements, earnings surprises, wars, or other high-conviction catalysts, it may choose Immediate Institutional Entry or another strategy.

That framing scales much better as the bot evolves because ORB becomes one tool in a toolbox rather than the central rule everything else has to work around.
